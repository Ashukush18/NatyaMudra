Bharatanatyam

A major form of Indian classical dance that originated in Tamil Nadu, known for its grace, purity, tenderness, and sculpturesque poses.

Sacred Dance

Tamil Nadu, India

Origin

The roots of a timeless tradition.

Bharatanatyam is a major form of Indian classical dance that originated in Tamil Nadu. Historically, Bharatanatyam was performed by female dancers in temples and later in courts. It is known for its grace, purity, tenderness, and sculpturesque poses. The dance is accompanied by Carnatic music and involves intricate rhythmic patterns and expressive storytelling.

c. 2nd Century BCE

Natyasastra

Sage Bharata Muni writes the Natyasastra, a comprehensive treatise on performing arts, laying the theoretical foundation for Indian classical dance forms.

Medieval Period

Temple Dancers (Devadasis)

The dance form, then known as Sadir, is primarily performed by Devadasis in the temples and courts of Southern India, particularly in Tamil Nadu.

Early 20th Century

Revival Movement

Figures like E. Krishna Iyer and Rukmini Devi Arundale lead a movement to revive and reform the dance, bringing it from temples to the public stage and renaming it 'Bharatanatyam'.

Mid-20th Century to Present

Global Recognition

Bharatanatyam gains international acclaim and is practiced and performed by artists worldwide, evolving with contemporary interpretations while preserving its traditional core.

Traditional Meaning

Symbolism woven into every movement.

Bharatanatyam is deeply rooted in Hindu mythology and spirituality. The dance often depicts stories from the Puranas and other religious texts, conveying themes of devotion, love, and the triumph of good over evil. The mudras (hand gestures) and facial expressions are used to communicate the narrative and emotional content of the dance.

Performance Context

From sacred temples to global stages.

Bharatanatyam is traditionally performed in temples during festivals and religious ceremonies. Today, it is also performed on stage in cultural events and concerts worldwide. The dance is typically performed solo by female dancers, although group performances are also common. The performance includes various elements such as Nritta (pure dance), Nritya (expressive dance), and Natya (dramatic dance).
